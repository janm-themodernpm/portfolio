## Design Context

### Users
**Primary audience**: Hiring managers and recruiters evaluating Jan Patrick McGhee for senior product management roles. They arrive from LinkedIn or a direct link, scan the page in 30-60 seconds, and decide whether to reach out. Secondary audience: fellow PMs and founders evaluating credibility for collaboration.

**Context**: Professional evaluation. The user is comparing this portfolio against dozens of others. They're scanning, not reading. Every section must earn its attention. The portfolio's job is to make them click "View My Work" or "LinkedIn" — not to be beautiful for its own sake.

**Job to be done**: Convince a skeptical hiring manager in under a minute that Jan ships real products, drives measurable business impact, and has the technical depth to lead ML/Trust & Safety platforms at scale.

### Brand Personality
**Three words**: Confident, precise, understated.

**Voice**: Direct and factual. Lets the work speak. No superlatives, no buzzwords, no "passionate about." Numbers over adjectives. Specificity over generality.

**Emotional goals**: The visitor should feel "this person is sharp and credible" — not "this website is cool." The design should be invisible in the best sense: it communicates competence without drawing attention to itself.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Visual tone**: Editorial magazine meets technical documentation. Think Stripe's clarity and Linear's precision — not their specific aesthetics, but their confidence in restraint. Every element earns its place.

**Palette**: "Ink & Paper" — warm ink-black substrate (#12110f), warm cream text (#ece8e1), slate blue accent (#5a7d9a) for authority, muted amber (#c49a4a) for measurable impact, sage green (#6a9a72) for active status, stone gray (#8a8578) for supporting text.

**Typography**: Instrument Serif for display headings (editorial weight), DM Sans for body (clean readability), DM Mono for labels and numbers (technical precision). The serif/sans pairing signals "editorial mind, engineering hands."

**Theme**: Dark mode only. Warm undertones throughout — the darkness should feel like ink on paper, not a terminal screen.

**References**: Stripe (clarity, confidence, technical precision), Linear (restraint, information density done well), Monocle (editorial typography, deliberate color).

**Anti-references**: AI-generated portfolios (neon on dark, purple gradients, glassmorphism, centered hero metrics). Generic SaaS templates. Anything that looks like it was built by dragging Tailwind components into a page.

### Design Principles

1. **Numbers speak louder than words.** Every metric is amber. Every bold figure draws the eye. The portfolio proves impact through data, not claims. If it can be quantified, quantify it.

2. **Restraint is confidence.** Two accent colors, not five. One serif, one sans, one mono. Subtle borders, not glowing gradients. The design whispers "I know exactly what I'm doing" — it never shouts.

3. **Editorial, not decorative.** Every design choice should feel like it was made by an editor, not a decorator. Typography creates hierarchy. Color carries meaning. Whitespace is intentional silence, not leftover space.

4. **Warm substrate, cool authority.** The background warmth (ink-black, cream text, amber accents) creates approachability. The cool blue accent creates trust. This tension — human warmth plus professional authority — is the brand.

5. **Scan first, read second.** A hiring manager will scan before they read. Section labels, metric numbers, role titles, and product badges must tell the story at scan speed. Body text is for the ones who stay.

### Technical Constraints
- Vanilla HTML/CSS/JS — no frameworks, no build tools
- Single-page site: index.html + style.css + main.js
- Google Fonts only (DM Mono, DM Sans, Instrument Serif)
- Mobile responsive at 375px minimum, breakpoint at 768px
- WCAG AA compliance (4.5:1 text contrast, 3:1 UI components)
- CSS custom properties for all design tokens
- IntersectionObserver for scroll animations (fire once, no re-trigger)
- prefers-reduced-motion should be respected

| Stone gray | --color-text-muted | Supporting text, inactive, foundational |
| Warm cream | --color-text | Primary content, headings |
